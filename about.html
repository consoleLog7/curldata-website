About CurlData

Bringing analytics to curling

Curling is often called “chess on ice” for good reason. Every shot, end, and decision requires strategy, patience, and precision. Yet despite the strategic depth of the game, curling has historically lagged behind other sports in the use of data, analytics, and evidence-based decision-making.

CurlData was created to help close that gap.

Founded in 2021 and continuously evolving ever since, CurlData is a data science and sport analytics initiative focused on making curling analytics more accessible, practical, and useful for athletes, coaches, teams, and fans. Through interactive tools, statistical research, and data-driven insights, CurlData explores the numbers behind the game and helps bring analytics to the broader curling community.

From the Founder

CurlData began from a simple observation: curling is one of the most strategic sports in the world, but many curlers are still forced to make important decisions with limited access to clear, practical data.

As a competitive curler with experience at the provincial, national, and university levels, CurlData’s founder saw firsthand how large the analytics gap was between curling and other major sports. When a research opportunity emerged, CurlData was launched with the belief that better data could help curlers better understand the game, evaluate strategy, and make more informed decisions on the ice.

Since that initial research project, CurlData has grown into an integrated platform for curling analytics, including interactive dashboarding, published statistics, research articles, and team-focused analytics services.

The goal is simple: give curlers access to analytics that are easy to understand, easy to use, and grounded in real game data. Whether it is exploring win rates, understanding end-by-end outcomes, or challenging traditional strategy with statistical evidence, CurlData exists to make curling analytics available to everyone.

Thank you for your interest in CurlData. We invite you to explore the platform, read our research, try the dashboard, connect with us on social media, or reach out directly with questions, ideas, or feedback.
